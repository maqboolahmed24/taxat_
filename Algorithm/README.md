## Shared Spine Vocabulary
- Canonical shell families are fixed before feature-level specialization: `CALM_SHELL`, `CLIENT_PORTAL_SHELL`, and `GOVERNANCE_DENSITY_SHELL`.
- Native windows and scenes are embodiments of those same shell families; where native delivery must be explicit, route-visible native contracts publish `surface_embodiment = NATIVE_OPERATOR` instead of inventing a second shell family.
- Route-stable shell and workspace identity uses the same field family across browser, native, portal, governance, and collaboration surfaces: `shell_family`, `object_anchor_ref`, `dominant_question`, `dominance_contract`, `settlement_state`, `recovery_posture`, `shell_stability_token`, `workspace_version`, and `view_guard_ref`.
- `shell_continuity_fuzz_harness` is the deterministic regression artifact for that shared shell
  spine. It enumerates browser and native perturbation cases, preserves shrinkable failure
  sequences, and proves that inline recovery does not silently remount a different shell, object,
  module, or action meaning for the same lawful object.
- `focus_restore_return_target_harness` is the paired deterministic regression artifact for
  close/back/help/stale-return behavior. It proves that keyboard-first focus restoration, lawful
  fallback order, and browser/native identifier parity stay bound to serialized anchors rather than
  browser history or local widget heuristics.
- `upload_session_recovery_harness` is the deterministic governed-upload recovery artifact for
  reconnect, reload, stale request rebase, duplicate retry, scanner delay, attachment
  confirmation, and cross-device continuation. It proves that frozen upload identity, storage
  reuse, explicit stale-rebind posture, and completion boundaries survive every lawful resume path.
- `native_cache_hydration_automation_pack` is the deterministic native persistence and UI artifact
  for pre-paint compatibility checks, selective purge, lawful scene restoration, and post-restore
  mutation gating across cold start, reconnect, tenant switch, downgrade, revocation, and schema
  drift.
- `semantic_accessibility_regression_pack` is the deterministic proof artifact for semantic anchor
  stability. It binds Playwright and XCUITest coverage to the exact semantic selector contract and
  proves keyboard-only, screen-reader, live-update, reduced-motion, and return-path behavior across
  governed shells.
- `low_noise_budget_audit` is the deterministic per-frame proof artifact for calm-shell surface
  count, copy load, duplicate-posture compression, and scan-load accounting.
- `low_noise_budget_audit_pack` is the paired deterministic scenario matrix for calm-shell first
  view, refresh, reconnect, and detail-fallback budget closure.
- The authoritative route-visible read models are `LowNoiseExperienceFrame`, `NativeOperatorWorkspaceScene`, `NativeOperatorSecondaryWindowScene`, `ClientPortalWorkspace`, `CustomerRequestListSnapshot`, `WorkspaceSnapshot`, `WorkInboxSnapshot`, `TenantGovernanceSnapshot`, `GovernancePolicySnapshot`, `PrincipalAccessView`, `RoleTemplateMatrix`, `AuthorityLinkInventoryItem`, `RetentionGovernanceFrame`, and `AuditInvestigationFrame`.
- `ClientPortalWorkspace`, `CustomerRequestListSnapshot`, and `WorkInboxSnapshot` SHALL publish top-level `settlement_state` and `recovery_posture` directly on the route-visible contract; portal-local freshness or queue-local status language does not replace the shared shell spine.
- Shared interaction posture is carried explicitly through `PortalInteractionLayer`, `OperatorInteractionLayer`, and `GovernanceInteractionLayer`; those layer contracts are the shared contract boundary between server-authored interaction semantics and platform-specific rendering, and queue/list routes inherit them rather than inventing route-local selector, motion, stale-view, restore, or current-vs-history rules.
- `InteractionLayerFoundationContract` is the shared root for `PortalInteractionLayer`, `OperatorInteractionLayer`, and `GovernanceInteractionLayer`; the README, schemas, and shell law all treat that foundation as the server-authored contract boundary rather than renderer-local behavior.
- `ClientPortalWorkspace`, `CustomerRequestListSnapshot`, and customer-visible `WorkspaceSnapshot.customer_request_workspace` SHALL also publish the shared `PortalLanguageContract`; client-safe wording, banned internal vocabulary, explicit due/current/history/settlement grammar, and bounded portal copy budgets are part of the server-authored route contract, not renderer-local editorial cleanup.
- Visibility class language is explicit shared-spine vocabulary: customer-safe and customer-visible projections stay distinct from internal-only, staff-full, governance-controlled, and authority-facing posture; no surface may silently widen those boundaries.

## Reference document roles
- `AUDIT_FINDINGS.md` states what historical material defects were found and whether each numbered finding is now closed.
- `PATCH_RESOLUTION_INDEX.md` records the concrete mechanism families, contract families, validator rules, and guard checks that keep those findings closed.
- `contract_integrity_requirements.md` records the standing ambiguities the architecture SHALL continue to close even after the historical findings are resolved.
- `constraint_traceability_register.json` is the machine-checkable live constraint register. Each live constraint entry binds named obligations to authoritative refs, validator/guard enforcement, downstream read surfaces, and vector coverage.
- `constraint_coverage_index.md` is the human summary of the live constraint register; it is not a second patch log, a second findings ledger, or a home for historical cleanup notes.
- `python3 Algorithm/scripts/validate_contracts.py --self-test` and `python3 Algorithm/tools/forensic_contract_guard.py`
  validate the live traceability spine directly: `constraint_traceability_register.json`, the
  active named constraint register in `constraint_coverage_index.md`, and the concrete file-plus-term
  anchors listed for each live named constraint must stay synchronized.
