## Outcome Notes

- The current posture is explicit and fail-closed rather than implied: `selection_status = PROVIDER_SELECTION_REQUIRED`, `managed_default_status = BLOCKED_BY_PLATFORM_PROVIDER_SELECTION`, and `topology_mode = TRANSACTIONAL_OUTBOX_AND_AUTHENTICATED_DEDUPE_INBOX_REBUILDABLE_TRANSPORT_ONLY_BROKER`.
- No broker platform was silently chosen. The pack freezes `4` lawful provider families, `5` environment rows, `5` channel-family rows, `14` concrete channel rows, `6` retry/dead-letter rows, `6` ordering/partitioning rows, and `6` idempotency/dedupe rows while leaving account, project, region, and credential specifics deferred until platform resolution.
- Broker semantics are now machine-readable instead of implicit. Stage orchestration, upload and evidence processing, authority send and ingress, external delivery/governance, and restore/rebuild each have explicit producers, consumers, persistence boundaries, replay truth, and duplicate-meaning basis.
- Durable truth remains outside the broker by construction. Every broker-mediated channel is bound back to durable outbox, inbox, control-store, audit-store, or object-store truth so broker loss remains recoverable instead of becoming a legal-state gap.
- Authority/provider callback ingress is now locked behind the required authenticated dedupe boundary: `channel.authority.callback.ingress` enters the inbox and correlation layer before any legal-state mutation or projection update can occur.
- Ordering is now explicit per family instead of globally assumed. Families that need per-run, per-submission, or per-object sequencing carry stable ordering and partition keys, while looser fan-out or rebuild flows remain intentionally non-strict to avoid fake guarantees.
- Dead-letter posture is inspectable instead of hand-waved. Poison or repeatedly failing messages route to named recovery paths with replay authority anchored in durable truth, so later workers cannot accidentally treat the broker queue itself as the source of record.
- The remaining gaps are explicit rather than hidden: platform provider selection is still unresolved, and broker credential material is still represented only as alias refs pending the later provider-adoption and secret-binding work.
