### Override And Progression Notes

- Overrideability is now centralized instead of being scattered through prose: `4` gate families are `NON_OVERRIDEABLE`, `4` allow direct scoped overrides, and `3` are override-dependent downstream gates that may only mirror already-documented upstream override posture.
- `ACCESS_GATE` is modeled as a special first-class gate family, but not as a `GateDecisionRecord`; the outputs preserve its distinct enum and pre-manifest exit behavior rather than flattening it into the non-access severity model.
- `TRUST_GATE` and `FILING_GATE` now explicitly preserve the bounded override rule: they may expose unresolved upstream override posture, but they do not invent new override families, and `FILING_GATE` can never be upgraded above `PASS_WITH_NOTICE` by an active override.
- The conditional late-chain exceptions are now machine-registered rather than implicit: `AMENDMENT_GATE` is tied only to `amendment_intent` and `amendment_submit`, is excluded from drift-preparation evaluation, and still allows the narrow early amendment-basis failure branch; `FILING_GATE` records both the ordinary `P15` path and the early filing-readiness branch in `P09`.
