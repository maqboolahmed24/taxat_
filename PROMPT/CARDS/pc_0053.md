- The current posture is explicit and fail-closed rather than implied: `selection_status = PROVIDER_SELECTION_REQUIRED`, `managed_default_status = BLOCKED_BY_PLATFORM_PROVIDER_SELECTION`, and `topology_mode = DISPOSABLE_SHARED_CACHE_PLUS_ROUTE_BOUND_RESUME_METADATA_WITH_STRICT_PARTITION_ISOLATION`.
- No cache platform was silently chosen. The pack freezes `4` lawful provider families, `5` environment rows, `5` governed family rows, `5` partition-key rows, `5` resume-binding rows, `5` family TTL rows, `10` invalidation rows, `10` local/shared boundary rows, and `5` contract-map rows while leaving concrete namespace creation, ACL wiring, encryption binding, and live secret material deferred until the broader platform decision is explicit.
- Cache identity is now machine-readable instead of folklore. Manifest experience, collaboration workspace, client portal workspace, upload-session recovery, and native hydration each bind tenant, session, access, masking, route, object, and any required visibility or preview dimensions in one authoritative contract rather than broad object-id shortcuts.
- Resume law is now explicit and secret-safe. Raw resume tokens remain transport material only; persisted state stores hashed envelope data, frontier, delivery-window posture, and invalidation reason instead of free-floating token strings.
- TTL never outruns invalidation. Tenant switch, client switch, privilege downgrade, masking tightening, route or object drift, session revocation, step-up completion, request rebase, schema incompatibility, and cache-only restoration now all resolve to explicit shared-cache, local-cache, resume-lineage, stale-render, and mutation-gate outcomes.
- Local versus shared cache responsibilities are now explicit instead of blurred. Shared projection snapshots and hashed resume envelopes remain allowed with strict partition identity, native disk hydration is bounded by purge and compatibility rules, and raw resume tokens, session cookies or bearers, and authority or provider credentials are all forbidden at rest.
- Cache-only restoration now stays visibly bounded. Browser and native restore paths may reopen read-only continuity when compatible, but mutation and filing remain blocked until live legality is re-established from durable truth.
- The remaining gaps are explicit rather than hidden: cache product selection is still unresolved, and runtime secret aliases remain logical refs only until a later provider-adoption pass binds a concrete managed service.
