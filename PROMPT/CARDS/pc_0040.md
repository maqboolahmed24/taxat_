### Outcome Notes

- Taxat now has one executable IdP policy pack that separates coarse provider posture from engine-owned legality:
  - `8` coarse roles
  - `18` scopes
  - `10` step-up or approval trigger rows
  - `6` explicit session profiles
- The role catalog stays intentionally shallow. It supports portal, operator, governance, audit, support, runtime, and bootstrap entry posture, but every role keeps delegation basis, `AuthorityLink`, authority-of-record outcome, and final approval legality in `engine_owned_dimensions`.
- Elevated scopes are now explicit request-time challenge hints rather than hidden prose. They never stand in for final command authorization, and service clients do not inherit human sign-off posture.
- MFA and step-up posture is now frozen as:
  - tenant-default MFA policy `NEVER_WITH_ACTION_DRIVEN_STEP_UP`
  - explicit factors `webauthn_roaming`, `otp`, and `recovery_code`
  - source-backed elevated-scope plus post-login Action challenge semantics
- Browser, native, machine, invite-upgrade, and governed-upload session law now lives side by side instead of being spread across the corpus. Native uses rotating refresh tokens, machine runtime stays outside human step-up rotation, and invalidation after step-up, tenant switch, logout, revocation, authority rebinding drift, stale-view rebase, and invite expiry is explicit.
- The `access-stepup-matrix` viewer now exposes the same policy pack in a governance-density inspection surface with:
  - semantic rail navigation for roles, scopes, and session profiles
  - compact action-family matrix rows
  - persistent right-hand inspector details
  - reduced-motion parity
  - explicit source refs without exposing raw provider secrets or turning the viewer into an authorization editor
- Provider-console drift is fail-closed. The flow persists a structured drift register instead of silently mutating mismatched live posture.
