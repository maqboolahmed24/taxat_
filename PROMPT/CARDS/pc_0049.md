## Outcome Notes

- The current canonical posture is explicit and fail-closed rather than implied: `selection_status = PROVIDER_SELECTION_REQUIRED` and `root_posture = LOGICAL_TOPOLOGY_FROZEN_PROVIDER_UNRESOLVED`.
- No cloud secret manager or KMS/HSM provider was silently chosen. Instead, the pack freezes `4` provider-stack options while keeping live resource IDs, policy document IDs, and platform-specific adoption steps deferred until the later platform decision is actually made.
- The pack now normalizes `20` alias families across `18` namespace roots, with `16` distinct namespace partitions touched by active alias families. `11` alias families are value-bearing and `7` are explicitly one-time-capture or provider-reveal secrets.
- The key hierarchy is now machine-readable instead of prose-only: `8` key families and `20` envelope bindings make the distinction between provider-managed defaults, customer-managed KMS roots, optional HSM-capable roots, runtime wrapping keys, authority wrapping keys, provider-ingest wrapping keys, bootstrap/tooling wrapping keys, and break-glass wrapping keys explicit.
- Least privilege is now frozen in one place through `10` actor roles and `10` explicit grants. CI and projection paths are metadata-only, support tooling is fenced away from authority secrets, rotation automation has write/rotate/revoke powers without becoming a standing read client, and break-glass remains dual-control and production-scoped.
- Rotation and revoke posture is now bound to concrete secret classes through `16` policy rows. Provider soft-delete windows are explicitly subordinated to Taxat revocation law: once Taxat marks a version revoked, provider recoverability does not restore active authorization.
- The earlier ordering tension remains explicit rather than hidden: `pc_0038` emitted HMRC vault-binding records before this root topology existed. This task closes the machine-readable topology gap without pretending that the dependency order never happened.
- During viewer verification, one real defect surfaced and was fixed: the alias-preview surface originally showed the first namespace bound to an alias instead of the namespace matching the selected environment. The ledger now renders environment-specific store/metadata previews correctly.
