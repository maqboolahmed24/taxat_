### Outcome Notes

- Taxat now has one authoritative transactional-email template and callback pack that normalizes:
  - `6` allowed customer-visible template families
  - `6` provider-event mapping rows
  - `3` callback endpoint records
  - `7` blocked internal-only or otherwise forbidden event families
  - `4` blocked provider-boundary identity or MFA mail families
- The allowed template families are now machine-readable rather than prose-only:
  - request-for-information creation
  - staff customer-visible comment creation
  - due-date change
  - item resolved or closed
  - portal help acknowledgement
  - support-contact acknowledgement
- Internal-only notes, assignments, escalations, internal attachments, audit-only events, and provider-owned identity mail remain explicitly blocked from Taxat customer-template semantics.
- Delivery callbacks are now frozen as evidence-only inputs. The event-mapping layer permits delivery evidence, suppression posture, retry posture, and observability counters, while explicitly forbidding any direct mutation of work-item truth, approval truth, or authority truth.
- Privacy-minimizing telemetry is now explicit instead of implied. Open and click tracking remain disabled by default, with any future enablement left as an explicit typed gap rather than a silent provider default.
- Callback hardening is now deterministic and resumable:
  - HTTPS callback URLs are environment-bound
  - callback auth is explicit and vault-ref based
  - replay protection and idempotency keys are machine-readable
  - duplicate provider deliveries normalize into single internal evidence updates
- The `notification-copy-atlas` viewer now provides one low-noise editorial inspection surface with:
  - a top context bar with stream, environment, and selected notification family
  - a left template rail
  - a central preview canvas with continuity and merge-variable provenance
  - a lifecycle rail from product event to internal evidence
  - a persistent right-hand inspector for callback posture, mapping, and privacy notes
  - reduced-motion parity
- Sandbox rendering and preview are explicit and safe. The checked-in inventory and fixture posture let later agents test templates without sending to real recipients.
