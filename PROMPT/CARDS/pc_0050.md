## Outcome Notes

- The current canonical posture is explicit and fail-closed rather than implied: `selection_status = PROVIDER_SELECTION_REQUIRED`, `provider_resolution_posture = PLATFORM_PROVIDER_SELECTION_REQUIRED`, and `topology_mode = SINGLE_CLUSTER_TWO_DATABASES_HARD_BOUNDARY`.
- No managed cloud provider was silently chosen. The pack instead freezes `2` lawful provider-service options, `5` environment rows, `4` store/profile rows, `12` role rows, `1` explicit membership edge, and `6` restore gates while leaving cloud-vendor product names and region identifiers deferred until platform selection is actually resolved.
- The control store and audit store are now mechanically separated rather than prose-only: `taxat_control` carries transactional truth plus migration and restore-verification metadata, while `taxat_audit` carries the partitioned append-only ledger and mutable stream-head support only.
- Append-only evidence law is now machine-readable and schema-bound: `pg_audit_append_writer` is the only insert role, `0` roles have update/delete grants on evidence rows, `audit_admin.guard_audit_event_insert()` enforces `audit_stream_ref + stream_sequence` monotonicity and `prev_event_hash`, and `audit_admin.reject_audit_event_mutation()` hard-rejects update/delete attempts.
- PITR posture is now concrete instead of implicit: authoritative environments freeze `wal_level = replica`, `archive_mode = on`, explicit base-backup cadence, restore verification, privacy reconciliation, queue rebuild, authority rebuild, and authority-binding revalidation before reopen.
- The viewer closes the remaining topology gap for later agents: one page now exposes the twin-ledger boundary, role model, and restore law without drifting into cloud-console noise or DBA metrics chrome.
